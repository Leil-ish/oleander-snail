Services Offered by Leila Anderson, LMFT-S

In-person and virtual sessions available across Texas.

Therapy

Feeling stuck in cycles of shame, fear, or self-doubt? If your past — especially experiences in faith-based or high-control environments — still shapes how you move through the world, you’re not alone. Many of my clients grapple with perfectionism, anxiety, and a deep sense of unworthiness that makes self-trust feel out of reach.

I specialize in helping people heal from spiritual trauma, complicated grief, and internalized shame. Through EMDR, DBT, and Internal Family Systems (IFS), I support clients in moving from judgment to compassion, from fear to clarity. Therapy here is about reclaiming your voice and building a life that fits you.

Areas of Focus

  • Spiritual Trauma & Religious Deconstruction – Reclaiming autonomy and healing fear-based conditioning.
  • Grief & Loss – Support through death, disconnection, or the loss of identity or belief systems.
  • Shame & Perfectionism – Loosening the grip of internalized criticism and scarcity.
  • Addiction & Recovery – Working with the intersections of trauma, identity, and recovery.

My rate is $200 per hour. I do not accept insurance, but I offer superbills and work with platforms like Mentaya to help you access out-of-network reimbursement. If cost is a concern, we can explore options in your free consultation.

Consultation

Are you a clinician, treatment program, or leader facing complex clinical challenges? I offer consultation rooted in lived experience, clinical rigor, and justice-oriented practice.

With over a decade of experience across psychiatric, forensic, and residential settings, I bring trauma-literate frameworks to help programs grow with integrity.

Consultation Services Include

  • Program and curriculum development for trauma and addiction recovery settings
  • Crisis response strategies for high-acuity environments
  • Case consultation for clients with religious trauma, shame, and identity conflict
  • Systemic review of policy through a trauma-informed lens

Clinical Supervision

As a Licensed Marriage and Family Therapist Supervisor (LMFT-S), I offer clinical supervision for associate therapists pursuing licensure in Texas.

My approach is relational, equity-rooted, and designed to support emerging therapists in cultivating a voice that’s clinically sound and fully their own. Supervision is a space to explore complexity with safety and rigor.

Supervision fees are discussed during an initial call and vary based on practice structure and experience level.

Speaking Engagements

I offer presentations on trauma recovery, systems of care, and the intersections of identity, ethics, and clinical work. My talks blend research, clinical application, and story — with warmth, clarity, and intention.

Popular Topics

  • The Long Shadow of Spiritual Trauma – Naming, understanding, and healing religious wounds
  • Trauma-Informed Crisis Response – De-escalation and support in high-stakes spaces
  • The Role of Perfectionism in Recovery – Shame, scarcity, and what gets left out of addiction narratives
  • Integrating DBT & IFS – Supporting trauma recovery with layered modalities

I’ve presented at national conferences, graduate programs, and clinical trainings. Let’s build something that speaks to your team or community.

Professional Training

Creating trauma-informed spaces takes more than language — it takes leadership, learning, and accountability. I design trainings that help teams implement real-world change with clarity and care.

Topics Include

  • Nonviolent crisis response for mental health settings
  • Supporting religious deconstruction and spiritual abuse recovery
  • Integrating DBT/IFS into addiction and trauma work
  • Neurodivergent-affirming care in recovery settings

Trainings can be one-time or ongoing and are customized to your team’s needs.
